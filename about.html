About me

Hi there, my name is Jacob Lewandowski and I am a graduate of the Wroclaw University of Science and Technology in Poland. I have searched far and wide for what I wanted to do in life career-wise since the age of 16. I've worked a dozen jobs and tried a plethora of activities just to find that special something that I would gravitate towards.


Thanks to a group of great friends of mine, I was introduced to the world of programming for the first time back in the middle of 2020. My journey began by learning Java as a hobby. The logical aspect of this language really grew on me, and because of that, I developed a deep passion for the art of combining one's creativity and problem-solving skills, that this profession requires to create astonishing applications.


I always tried to code every day or learn how to do it whenever I could find some spare time, even if I was on a train on the way home I'd watch youtube videos, listen to podcasts, or read about the expert's view on a particular subject, tricks, or tech-stack that I was currently learning. After my graduation in July of 2021, I've decided to dive deep into the world of programming. Since then I picked up a few more technologies and made the decision to become a full-stack software developer.


Being a self-taught developer truly is a great test of one's discipline, willpower, and determination. Like every other programmer out there I have had my share of ups and downs on this path, but what always kept me moving forward was the chase for that feeling of accomplishment when I finally figured out the solution to my coding problem.


I am the type of person that at the end of the day wants to go to sleep knowing that I improved and became much better compared to yesterday, and I think that this is the exact reason why I fell in love with programming. It constantly pushes me to challenge myself and to learn how to overcome those problems. The best part of it is that the learning process never stops, so there is no room for stagnation, but lots of it for development.


"Our very survival depends on our ability to stay awake, to adjust to new ideas, to remain vigilant and to face the challenge of change." -Martin Luther King Jr.
